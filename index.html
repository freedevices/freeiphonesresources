Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about free government devices

Applying is simple! First, check your eligibility using our tool above. If you qualify through programs like Medicaid, SNAP, or based on income, you can apply directly through approved providers like AirTalk Wireless or TruConnect. You'll need to provide proof of eligibility (such as a benefits award letter or tax return) and complete a short application. Most applications are approved within 24-48 hours.

Yes! Participation in the SNAP program (EBT) automatically qualifies you for the Affordable Connectivity Program, which includes free or heavily discounted tablets. You'll need to provide a copy of your EBT card or approval letter when applying. Many providers offer specific tablet options for EBT participants with fast-track approval processes that take as little as 24 hours.

Approval times vary by provider but typically take 1-3 business days once all documentation is submitted. After approval, devices are shipped within 3-7 business days via priority mail. Some providers like Easy Wireless offer expedited processing for EBT and Medicaid participants, with devices arriving in as little as 3-5 days after application submission.

Absolutely! Many denials happen because of incomplete documentation or applying through the wrong provider. We recommend reviewing our eligibility checklist carefully and applying with a different provider if you were previously denied. Program requirements have also expanded in 2025, so you may now qualify even if you didn't before. Our eligibility checker can help determine your current qualification status.

There's no catch! The devices are completely free through the federal Affordable Connectivity Program. You will never be charged for the device itself. The program also provides a monthly discount of up to $30 on your service plan ($75 for tribal lands). Some providers offer completely free service plans, while others offer discounted plans starting at $0/month for eligible households. There are no hidden fees or contracts.

Yes, many households qualify for both a free smartphone and a free tablet. The program allows one device per eligible person in the household. For example, a family of four could potentially receive four devices (combination of phones and tablets). Each provider has slightly different policies, so we recommend checking with your chosen provider about their specific device offerings.

You'll need proof of identity (government-issued ID), proof of address (utility bill, lease agreement), and proof of program participation or income. For program participation, this could be your benefits award letter, EBT card (with your name), Medicaid card, or other official documentation. For income qualification, you can use your tax return, paycheck stubs, or Social Security benefit statements. The exact requirements vary slightly by provider.

The free or discounted service continues as long as you remain eligible and the Affordable Connectivity Program is funded. You'll need to recertify your eligibility annually. The device itself is yours to keep forever, regardless of future eligibility. If your eligibility status changes, you may need to switch to a low-cost plan, but you'll never lose your device.
